MARCH 2026 NEWSLETTER
FEBRUARY SUMMARY
After our biggest month yet, we had a quieter month in February. It was kinda nice. Sometimes, it can feel like there is this imperative to do more and more every month and every year. It’s nice to remind ourselves that we don’t have to. While helping our neighbors is important, it is a good reminder that we are all neighbors and need to take care of ourselves. Since 2017, it’s been a rotating, ragtag crew of a couple dozen volunteers making this happen. The work will only continue if it’s sustainable. If we want to keep showing up for one another, we need to remember that caring for ourselves is not separate from caring for our neighbors.
With that being said, there were some moments to highlight. We had some great, new faces helping out at events this month and the return of a day one (hi Kyle!). The 2nd Saturday event at Oak City Cares was bustling with 6 walk-up repairs, as others worked on some real fixer-uppers to distribute to folks there and at the South Wilmington Street Men’s Shelter. Our shop hour folks were not deterred by the cold rain or the cramped conditions. The vibes were high as they stripped some damaged bikes for parts and fixed up others for distribution.
That's all for now. Thanks to everyone who helped out and hope to see you at an event in March!
